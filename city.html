Marrakech The pearl of the south...

Marrakech is undoubtedly the most exotic city in Morocco.
It has always been appreciated for its particularity.
Arab poets called it "the pearl of the south, coming out of the Atlas".
It is also nicknamed "the red city" or "the city of gardens".
In winter, one admires the snow-capped peaks of the Atlas on the southern horizon and in summer the city burns under the desert sun.
Marrakech is clearly divided into two different cities:
the Medina protected by a great wall and the new city, Gueliz.
Gueliz offers all the possibilities of a modern city.

was founded as a commercial and artisanal center by an ancient Berber population that dominated southern Morocco.
It is one of the four royal cities (Fez, Meknes, Rabat and Marrakech)
and offers many buildings and monuments that document its history.
The Medina of Marrakech is surrounded by a large earthen wall of 20 km,
rust-colored that turns purple in the light of the setting sun.
A coach ride along the walls at sunset is a unique experience
